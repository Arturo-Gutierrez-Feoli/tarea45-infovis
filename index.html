Introduction

This page presents an interactive analysis of global video game sales data sourced from VGChartz. The dataset covers sales figures (in millions of units) across major gaming platforms and genres, segmented by geographic region: North America, Europe, and Japan.

All visualizations are implemented as SVG files using the ODViz (Open Data Visualization) format, which embeds the underlying data directly within each SVG image. This enables interactive features such as hover highlighting and bidirectional linking between visual elements and their corresponding data rows.

Problem: The video game industry generates massive sales data across multiple dimensions (genre, region, platform, year). Understanding which genres dominate, how regional preferences differ, and how the market evolved over time requires multi-dimensional visualization.

Data Source

VGChartz global sales database. Data aggregated by genre and region for the period 2000–2013.

Unidimensional

1. Sales by Genre — Bar Chart

Distribution of total global sales across eight major game genres

Analysis

This bar chart shows the total global sales (in millions of units) for each major video game genre. It is a unidimensional visualization — the only variable being analyzed is total sales, distributed across the nominal category of genre.

  • Action dominates with 481.8M units, nearly 28% of all sales analyzed.
  • Sports and Shooter follow as the second and third largest genres.
  • There is a notable drop after the top three: RPG, Platform and Misc form a mid-tier cluster around 193–205M.
  • Racing and Fighting are tied at 105.5M, roughly half of Action's volume.
Unidimensional

2. Market Share by Platform — Pie Chart

Proportional distribution of sales across major gaming platforms

Analysis

The pie chart displays the market share percentage for each major platform. This is a unidimensional visualization of the nominal variable "platform" measured by proportion of total sales.

  • PS2 leads with 22.3% of total sales, reflecting its exceptional 12-year library.
  • Xbox 360, PS3 and Wii form a cluster between 16–17%, indicating a highly competitive generation.
  • The DS handheld accounts for 14.3%, showing the relevance of portable gaming.
  • No single platform captures more than 25%, suggesting a fragmented but competitive market.
Unidimensional

3. Sales by Year of Release — Histogram

Annual evolution of global video game sales volume from 2000 to 2013

Analysis

This histogram visualizes how total global sales changed year by year for games released between 2000 and 2013. It shows the distribution of a single quantitative variable (sales) over the ordinal dimension of time.

  • Sales grew steadily from 2000 to 2007, peaking at 332.1M units.
  • 2007–2008 represent the golden era, coinciding with the Wii, Xbox 360 and PS3 being at peak popularity.
  • A consistent decline from 2009 to 2013 reflects the market transitioning toward digital distribution and mobile gaming.
  • The 2013 figure (55.2M) appears low as it only captures physical retail data before the generation shifted.
Bidimensional

4. North America vs Europe Sales — Scatter Plot

Relationship between NA and EU sales across genres

Analysis

The scatter plot shows the relationship between two quantitative variables: North America sales (X axis) and Europe sales (Y axis), with each point representing a genre. The dashed diagonal represents equal sales in both regions.

  • Action, Sports and Shooter are clearly above the diagonal — North America dominates these genres.
  • RPG is the notable exception: it falls above the diagonal, meaning Europe and Japan outperform NA, suggesting cultural preference for narrative-driven games.
  • Racing sits close to the diagonal, indicating balanced appeal between NA and EU.
  • Strategy and Simulation have modest but relatively balanced sales between regions.
Bidimensional

5. Sales by Genre and Region — Grouped Bar Chart

Comparison of NA, EU and Japan sales for the top five genres

Analysis

This grouped bar chart represents two categorical variables simultaneously: genre (groups) and region (bar color within each group). It reveals how regional preferences shape sales for each genre category.

  • North America (blue) leads in Action, Sports and Shooter by a wide margin.
  • Japan (red) is strongest in RPGs, nearly matching Europe — a cultural pattern consistent with the popularity of series like Final Fantasy and Dragon Quest.
  • Shooter shows the most extreme regional skew: NA is 202.5M vs Japan's near-zero 3.2M.
  • Platform games are the most geographically balanced among the top genres.
Multidimensional

6. Multidimensional Sales Analysis — Bubble Chart

Five simultaneous variables: NA sales, EU sales, total sales, genre, and peak year

Analysis

The bubble chart encodes five variables simultaneously: NA sales (X), EU sales (Y), total global sales (bubble size), genre (color and label), and peak year (text inside bubble).

  • Action occupies the top-right corner with the largest bubble: high in both NA and EU, and the highest total sales.
  • RPG shows a contrasting position: low NA sales but high EU sales, and its peak was in 2000 — the early PS2 era.
  • Shooter peaked in 2008, coinciding with Call of Duty: Modern Warfare's cultural impact.
  • Genres with smaller bubbles (Strategy, Simulation, Fighting) cluster in the lower-left, confirming their niche status globally.
  • The chart reveals that NA is the primary driver of total global sales for most genres, with the notable exception of RPG.

Conclusions

The analysis of global video game sales reveals several consistent patterns across all six visualizations:

  • Action dominates globally, but this is largely driven by North American demand. The genre's lead is structural across all chart types.
  • Strong regional preferences exist: Japan favors RPGs, North America favors Shooters, and Europe shows more balanced interest. No single genre appeals uniformly worldwide.
  • The market peaked in 2007–2008, driven by the Wii's mass-market appeal and the maturity of the Xbox 360 and PS3 libraries.
  • Platform concentration is moderate: the top six platforms split the market fairly evenly (14–22% each), suggesting healthy competition.
  • The bubble chart confirms that high total sales require strong NA performance — with RPG as the main exception, driven by Japan.
